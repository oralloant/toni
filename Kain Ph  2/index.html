Filipino Street Food




Filipinos have a sweet tooth as well.
So here's a couple of sweet Pinoy street foods.




3. Taho



The taho is made from soy beans. They are soft, pudding-like dessert sold by a manong roaming around the streets.
It is serve with sago, and flavored with either strawberry syrup, ube syrup or caramel syrup called arnibal.





4. Tupig



Made from sticky rice with shredded coconut meat. The tupig is wrapped in banana leaves and cooked in a traditional
charcoal grill made specifically for tupig. When ready, they are warm, gooey and sweet.
